Kalpana Chawla

"The path from dreams to success does exist. May you have the vision to find it, the courage to get onto it, and the perseverance to follow it."

Born in Karnal, India in 1962, Kalpana Chawla became the first woman of Indian origin to travel to space. A mission specialist and robotic arm operator, she flew aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia and logged over 376 hours in space across two missions. Her work in computational fluid dynamics and her relentless pursuit of aerospace excellence made her a role model for millions around the world.

On February 1, 2003, Columbia disintegrated during re-entry into Earth's atmosphere. Kalpana Chawla and her six crewmates lost their lives. She remains one of the most celebrated astronauts in history — a symbol of scientific ambition and the courage to reach beyond every horizon.

1962 Born in Karnal, Haryana, India
1984 B.E. in Aeronautical Engineering, Punjab Engineering College
1988 PhD in Aerospace Engineering, University of Colorado

2003 STS-107 — Columbia mission, final flight
